Aritzia Mobile App UX Research – Enhancing Mobile Fashion Experience

This comprehensive UX research project explores the potential of a mobile app for Aritzia, a leading Canadian fashion brand for young women (15–45). While Aritzia is successful in-store and online, the absence of a mobile app creates a competitive disadvantage compared to brands like H&M and Zara. Our research aimed to evaluate customer needs, identify pain points, and provide actionable recommendations for app development.

The project was conducted by Liu, Wenxi (Vincent), Malhotra, Shivani, Nguyen, Dao (Hana), Tahiri, Anosha, and Yang, Dan, using a mixed-method approach that combined quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews. Through extensive research and user testing, we identified key opportunities for enhancing customer experience and supporting Aritzia's business goals including increasing sales, boosting satisfaction, and strengthening brand recognition.

Project Overview

Project Title: Aritzia Mobile App UX Research – Enhancing Mobile Fashion Experience

Objective: Evaluate customer needs and provide actionable recommendations for Aritzia mobile app development

Scope: Mixed-method research approach targeting mobile shopping preferences and pain points

Target Users: Women aged 18–35 who regularly shop on mobile, value convenience, and align with Aritzia's brand values

Research Goals: Understand how a mobile app could enhance customer experience, increase sales, boost satisfaction, and strengthen brand recognition while addressing competitive disadvantages in the mobile-first fashion market.

Research & Data:

Research Methods:

  • Mixed-Method Approach – Combined quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews
  • Google Forms Surveys – Comprehensive data collection on mobile shopping preferences
  • One-on-One Interviews – Deep insights into user behaviors and pain points
  • Collaborative Analysis – Team analysis using FigJam and MS Teams
  • Persona Development – Created detailed user personas based on research findings

Research Timeline:

  • Phase 1 (May 2025): Research planning and methodology development
  • Phase 2 (June 2025): Data collection and implementation
  • Phase 3 (July 2025): Insights crafting and analysis
  • Phase 4 (August 2025): Reflection and recommendations

Participant Profile:

  • Sample Size: 21 women, ages 18–44, with 62% aged 18–24
  • Completion Rate: 100% survey completion rate
  • Shopping Behavior: 72% shop via mobile (41% app, 31% browser)
  • Market Opportunity: 45% do not currently shop at Aritzia
  • Priority: 65% prioritize mobile convenience in shopping

Initial User Personas:

  • Sara (21): Trendy university student who prefers mobile shopping for convenience and style discovery
  • Ricky (29): Occasional shopper focused on quality basics with selective purchasing behavior
  • Ella (34): Regular customer who combines online and in-store shopping for comprehensive experience

Refined User Personas:

  • Emily (23): Weekly mobile shopper seeking convenience, personalized recommendations, and seamless checkout
  • Joanna (35): Values omnichannel experience, real-time stock updates, exclusive promotions, and integrated shopping journey

Key Findings & Pain Points:

Current Shopping Challenges:

  • Lack of Personalization: Users desire tailored shopping experiences and AI-powered recommendations
  • Inconsistent Experience: Gap between online and offline shopping experiences
  • Slow Interfaces: Performance issues affecting user satisfaction and conversion
  • Complex Checkout: Multi-step processes creating friction and abandonment
  • Limited Post-Purchase Engagement: Insufficient tracking and communication after purchase

User Needs & Design Solutions:

  • Seamless Checkout: One-step checkout with Apple Pay/PayPal integration for frictionless purchases
  • Personalized Journey: AI-powered homepage with customized product suggestions and style recommendations
  • Real-Time Updates: Enhanced post-purchase tracking with live order status and delivery notifications
  • Exclusive Engagement: App-only promotions, early access to collections, and member benefits
  • Omnichannel Integration: Seamless connection between app, website, and in-store experiences

Recommendations & Implementation:

  • Personalized Homepage: Implement AI-driven content curation based on browsing history and preferences
  • Simplified Checkout: Integrate one-touch payment options with secure, streamlined purchase flow
  • Enhanced Tracking: Provide real-time order updates with detailed delivery information
  • Mobile-First Features: Develop app-exclusive promotions and member benefits to drive adoption
  • Smart Recommendations: Deploy machine learning algorithms for personalized style suggestions

Future Vision - "Aritzia ProLab":

  • AI Integration: Mood-responsive AR previews that adapt to user preferences and occasions
  • Conversational Styling: AI-powered styling assistants for personalized fashion advice
  • Holographic Fitting: Virtual fitting suites using advanced holographic technology
  • Smart Wardrobe: Intelligent wardrobe syncing for outfit coordination and style planning
  • Immersive Experience: AR/VR integration for virtual fashion shows and product interactions

Project Impact & Insights:

The research demonstrates a clear demand for an Aritzia mobile app among target users. Key insights reveal a mobile-first mindset with strong demand for personalization, frictionless shopping, and omnichannel integration. Users emphasize the need for simplified checkout processes, personalized recommendations, and real-time order tracking as critical app functionalities.

By addressing personalization, checkout efficiency, real-time tracking, and exclusive mobile features, the proposed app can strengthen customer loyalty, enhance brand experience, and position Aritzia competitively in the mobile-first fashion market. The research provides a solid foundation for developing a user-centered mobile application that meets both customer needs and business objectives.
